So You Have Some Extra Money To Burn On Pole

A guide to investing in your pole journey — from intensive camps to one-on-one coaching and everything in between.

So you've been bitten by the pole bug, you've got some disposable income, and you're wondering how to level up your skills beyond your regular studio classes. Whether you've got a little extra to spend or you're ready to go all in, here are some ways to invest in your pole journey.

🏕️ Pole Camps

Pole camps are immersive multi-day experiences where you train intensively with world-class instructors, often in beautiful destinations. They're part vacation, part pole intensive, and 100% unforgettable.

What to Expect

  • Multiple workshops per day across various styles (tricks, flow, exotic, flexibility)
  • Training with internationally renowned instructors
  • Meeting pole dancers from around the world
  • Often held in resort-style locations with accommodation included
  • Prices typically range from €500 - €2000+ depending on location and duration

Things to Consider

  • Intensity level: Some camps are beginner-friendly, others expect intermediate+ skills
  • Style focus: Make sure the instructors and workshop styles align with your interests
  • Travel costs: Factor in flights, travel insurance, and any meals not included
  • Recovery time: Your body will need rest after — don't book anything intense right after!

💡 Tip: Follow your favorite instructors on Instagram — they often announce camp appearances and early-bird pricing there first.

👤 Private Classes

One-on-one instruction is one of the fastest ways to progress. A good private lesson is tailored entirely to your goals, your body, and your learning style.

Benefits of Privates

  • 100% focus on your specific goals and problem areas
  • Immediate feedback and corrections
  • Work on moves or combos at your own pace
  • Great for overcoming fear blocks or nailing competition pieces
  • Flexible scheduling (often available outside regular class times)

How to Get the Most Out of Privates

  • Come prepared: Have a list of moves or goals you want to work on
  • Record everything: Ask permission to film — you'll forget details otherwise
  • Be specific: "I want to clean up my Jade" is better than "teach me something cool"
  • Follow up: Practice what you learned before your next session

Pricing

Private lessons typically range from €40-150+ per hour depending on the instructor's experience and location. Guest instructors and competition winners often charge premium rates — and are often worth it for specialized skills.

💡 Tip: Consider splitting a private with a friend at a similar level — you'll save money and still get plenty of individual attention.

🏆 Competition Coaching

If you're thinking about competing, dedicated competition coaching can make the difference between showing up and standing out. A good coach helps with everything from choreography to mental prep.

What Competition Coaches Help With

  • Choreography creation and refinement
  • Trick selection based on scoring criteria
  • Performance quality — facials, presence, musicality
  • Video review and detailed feedback
  • Competition strategy and category selection
  • Mental preparation and nerves management

Types of Coaching

  • In-person coaching: Best for hands-on choreography work and trick refinement
  • Online coaching: More accessible, great for feedback on recorded run-throughs
  • Intensive packages: Multiple sessions over weeks/months leading up to comp
  • One-off consultations: Get feedback on an existing piece before you compete

Finding the Right Coach

  • Look at their competitive experience and judging credentials
  • Check if their style aligns with your vision
  • Ask for testimonials from past competitors they've coached
  • Make sure they understand the specific competition's rules and judging criteria

💡 Tip: Start working with a coach 3-6 months before your competition. Last-minute coaching rarely allows enough time to polish a piece properly.

💻 Online Studio Subscriptions

Online pole platforms give you access to hundreds (sometimes thousands) of tutorials from top instructors worldwide. Perfect for home practice, supplementing studio classes, or learning from specific artists you admire.

What You Get

  • On-demand tutorials for tricks, combos, and choreography
  • Often organized by level, style, and instructor
  • Flexibility training, conditioning, and off-pole workouts
  • New content added regularly
  • Learn from instructors you might never meet in person

Popular Platforms

  • Studio Veena — One of the OG online pole platforms
  • Open Dance Academy — Huge library, great for exotic and flow
  • Pole & Aerial Online — Variety of instructors and styles
  • Individual instructor platforms — Many top dancers have their own subscription sites

Getting the Most From Subscriptions

  • Set goals: Don't just browse randomly — pick a skill to work on
  • Take notes: Write down cues that help you remember technique
  • Practice deliberately: Watch, drill, record yourself, compare, repeat
  • Use the community: Many platforms have forums or groups for feedback

Pricing

Most subscriptions range from €10-40/month, with discounts for annual plans. Some offer free trials — use them to explore before committing!

💡 Tip: Check if your local studio offers any partnerships with online platforms — some have discount codes for their members.

Final Thoughts

There's no "right" way to spend money on pole — it all depends on your goals, learning style, and what excites you. Whether it's the adventure of a pole camp, the personalized attention of privates, the focused preparation of competition coaching, or the convenience of online learning, each option offers something unique.

The best investment? The one that keeps you excited to get on the pole. 💜
